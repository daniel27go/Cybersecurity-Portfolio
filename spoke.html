Daniel Ige – Governance, Risk & Compliance Journey

I am transitioning into cybersecurity with a focus on Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC). My background in epidemiology, data management, and grant oversight has given me a strong foundation in risk thinking, data protection, and regulatory compliance. This portfolio highlights how those experiences are shaping my path into cybersecurity.

My Story

My journey into cybersecurity did not begin with firewalls or network logs; it began with a deep commitment to protecting people, data, and the systems organizations rely on every day.

I started my career as an epidemiologist, analyzing large datasets, identifying risk patterns, and supporting public health interventions through statistical modeling. This work taught me how to handle sensitive information responsibly and communicate insights that influence critical decisions.

As a data manager, I built departmental databases, developed reporting dashboards, established data standards, and participated in HIPAA compliance efforts. These responsibilities deepened my understanding of data governance and secure data handling.

Most recently, as a grant manager overseeing more than $6 million in awards, I focused heavily on compliance, internal controls, and risk monitoring. I coordinated spending activities, conducted fraud and vendor due diligence, ensured regulatory alignment, and prepared dashboards and reports for leadership and auditors.

These experiences collectively inspired my pivot into cybersecurity and GRC. I realized that protecting data, assessing risk, enforcing standards, monitoring compliance, and supporting informed decision-making are core to both my past roles and the GRC function.
